Favorite Books

12 rules for Life is an important book for the 21st century west were secularism has left many without a value structure.

Hal Clements's "A Mission of Gravity" takes place on an alien planet where the gravity is hundreds of times stronger than Earth's. The native lifeHumans come to reasearch the planet and eventually teach the native life-forms to overcome their fear of heights through scientific thinking. Gravity here is symbolic of ignorance, enforcing the idea that all suffering is due to ignorance.

The Gulag Archipelago is a powerful story about possible societal outcomes if an ideaology becomes too powerful and also a story of the triumph of the individual.

Brave New World paints a picture of possible societal outcomes if we all become too distracted with worldly pleasures.

Zero to One: after labor and assets, the best way to make money is to start a company. How do you do that? Capitalism rewards those who do things ten times better than the next alternative.

Waking Up: a guide to a practice of mindfulness.

Deep Work: a manual on mindfulness applied to work.

Amusing Ourselves to Death asserts that people in general have an infinite appetite for distraction and that some things need careful consideration.

"Never Forget an Idea Again with The Feynman Technique" is an article by Scott Young that outlines the most basic ideas for using the method used by "The Great Explainer" Richard Feynman to learn and update physics. This is essential reading for the 21st century knowledge worker.
